# 🌟 MONTH 1 — Foundations I: Pinyin, Tones & Greetings

## 1.1 What is Mandarin Chinese?

Mandarin Chinese (普通话 Pǔtōnghuà) is the official language of China and one of the most widely spoken languages in the world. It is a **tonal language**, meaning the pitch of your voice changes the meaning of a word.

Chinese uses **characters (汉字 Hànzì)** for writing, but we will learn **Pinyin** first — the phonetic system using the Latin alphabet that helps us read and pronounce Chinese.

---

## 1.2 🎵 The Pinyin System (拼音 Pīnyīn)

Pinyin is the official romanization system for Chinese. Every Chinese syllable = **Initial (声母) + Final (韵母) + Tone (声调)**.

## 1.3 🎵 The Four Tones (四声 Sì Shēng)

Chinese has **4 tones** plus a neutral tone. The same sound with different tones = completely different words!

| Tone | Mark | Description | Example |
|------|------|-------------|---------|
| 1st tone | ā | High and flat | 妈 mā (mom) |
| 2nd tone | á | Rising (like a question) | 麻 má (hemp/numb) |
| 3rd tone | ǎ | Dipping (low, then up) | 马 mǎ (horse) |
| 4th tone | à | Falling sharply | 骂 mà (to scold) |
| Neutral | a | Short and light | 吗 ma (question particle) |

🎯 **Famous example — mā, má, mǎ, mà, ma:**
- 妈妈骑马，马慢，妈妈骂马。
- Māma qí mǎ, mǎ màn, māma mà mǎ.
- *Mom rides a horse. The horse is slow. Mom scolds the horse.*

### Tone Rules
1. When two 3rd tones appear together, the first changes to 2nd tone: 你好 nǐ hǎo → pronounced níhǎo
2. 一 (yī, one) and 不 (bù, not) change tones based on what follows them.

### 💬 Dialogue 1: First Meeting (第一次见面)

> **A:** 你好！我叫李明。你呢？
> Nǐ hǎo! Wǒ jiào Lǐ Míng. Nǐ ne?
> Hello! My name is Li Ming. And you?
>
> **B:** 你好！我叫Sarah。很高兴认识你！
> Nǐ hǎo! Wǒ jiào Sarah. Hěn gāoxìng rènshi nǐ!
> Hello! My name is Sarah. Nice to meet you!
>
> **A:** 我也很高兴认识你！你是哪国人？
> Wǒ yě hěn gāoxìng rènshi nǐ! Nǐ shì nǎ guó rén?
> Nice to meet you too! Which country are you from?
>
> **B:** 我是美国人。你呢？
> Wǒ shì Měiguó rén. Nǐ ne?
> I am American. And you?
>
> **A:** 我是中国人。欢迎你来中国！
> Wǒ shì Zhōngguó rén. Huānyíng nǐ lái Zhōngguó!
> I am Chinese. Welcome to China!

- 你是… (Nǐ shì…) — You are…
- 他/她是… (Tā shì…) — He/She is…

## 1.5 ✏️ Month 1 Exercises

1. **Tone Practice:** Say each word aloud: mā, má, mǎ, mà. Can you hear the difference?
2. **Write in Pinyin:** How do you say "Good morning" and "Goodbye" in Chinese?
3. **Fill in the blank:** 我___中国人。(I am Chinese.)
4. **Dialogue practice:** Introduce yourself using the dialogue above. Change the names and country!
5. **Tone marks:** Add the correct tone mark: ni hao (hello) → ?

---

## 🌏 Cultural Note: Why Tones Matter

In Chinese, the wrong tone can completely change your meaning. For example:
- 买 mǎi = to buy | 卖 mài = to sell
- 问 wèn = to ask | 吻 wěn = to kiss

Don't worry — with practice, tones become natural!

🎯 **Fun fact:** Chinese uses 万 (wàn = 10,000) as a unit, unlike English!
- 50,000 = 五万 wǔ wàn (5 × 10,000)
- 1,000,000 = 一百万 yī bǎi wàn (100 × 10,000)

### Special usage of 两 (liǎng)
When counting things (using measure words), use 两 liǎng instead of 二 èr for "2":
- 两个人 liǎng gè rén — two people
- 两本书 liǎng běn shū — two books

### 💬 Dialogue 3: Talking About Family (谈家庭)

> **A:** 你家有几口人？
> Nǐ jiā yǒu jǐ kǒu rén?
> How many people are in your family?
>
> **B:** 我家有五口人：爸爸、妈妈、一个哥哥、一个妹妹和我。
> Wǒ jiā yǒu wǔ kǒu rén: bàba, māma, yī gè gēge, yī gè mèimei hé wǒ.
> There are five people in my family: dad, mom, one older brother, one younger sister, and me.
>
> **A:** 你哥哥多大了？
> Nǐ gēge duō dà le?
> How old is your older brother?
>
> **B:** 他二十五岁。我今年二十岁。
> Tā èrshíwǔ suì. Wǒ jīnnián èrshí suì.
> He is 25 years old. I am 20 this year.

### Grammar: Age
- 你多大？Nǐ duō dà? — How old are you? (casual)
- 您多大年纪了？Nín duō dà niánjì le? — How old are you? (respectful, for elders)
- 我二十岁。Wǒ èrshí suì. — I am 20 years old.

# 🌟 MONTH 11 — Culture, Festivals & Emotions

## 11.1 Traditional Chinese Festivals (传统节日)

### Spring Festival / Chinese New Year (春节 Chūnjié)
- **Date:** 1st day of the 1st lunar month (usually Jan–Feb)
- **Activities:** Family reunion dinner, red envelopes (红包 hóngbāo), fireworks, lion dance
- **Greetings:** 新年快乐 (Xīnnián kuàilè) — Happy New Year! | 恭喜发财 (Gōngxǐ fācái) — Wishing you prosperity!

### Mid-Autumn Festival (中秋节 Zhōngqiū Jié)
- **Date:** 15th day of the 8th lunar month (usually Sep–Oct)
- **Activities:** Eating mooncakes (月饼 yuèbǐng), admiring the full moon, family gatherings
- **Greeting:** 中秋节快乐！(Zhōngqiū Jié kuàilè!)

### Dragon Boat Festival (端午节 Duānwǔ Jié)
- **Date:** 5th day of the 5th lunar month (usually May–June)
- **Activities:** Dragon boat racing, eating sticky rice dumplings (粽子 zòngzi)

### Qingming Festival (清明节)
- **Date:** April 4–6
- **Purpose:** Ancestors' remembrance, tomb-sweeping

## 11.3 Chinese Customs & Etiquette (礼仪习俗)

🍽️ **Dining etiquette:**
- Always wait for elders to eat first.
- Pouring tea for others before yourself is polite.
- Refusing food once or twice before accepting is common practice.
- Leaving a little food shows the host provided plenty.

🎁 **Gift-giving:**
- Don't give clocks (送钟 = sòng zhōng, similar to 送终 "attending a funeral").
- Don't give pears (梨 lí sounds like 离 "separation").
- Red and gold are lucky colors. White and black are for funerals.

🤝 **Greetings:**
- A slight nod or handshake is common.
- "Have you eaten?" (你吃了吗？Nǐ chī le ma?) is a common greeting showing care.

# 🌟 MONTH 12 — Review, Fluency & Next Steps

## 12.1 Grammar Review

### Key Grammar Patterns

**1. The "把" (bǎ) structure — putting the object before the verb:**
- 我把书放在桌子上了。Wǒ bǎ shū fàng zài zhuōzi shàng le. — I put the book on the table.

**2. The "被" (bèi) structure — passive voice:**
- 钱包被人偷了。Qiánbāo bèi rén tōu le. — The wallet was stolen.

**3. Complement of result:**
- 我学会了。Wǒ xuéhuì le. — I've learned it (successfully).
- 他跑累了。Tā pǎo lèi le. — He ran until he was tired.

**4. 又…又… (yòu…yòu…) — both…and…**
- 这个菜又好吃又便宜。Zhège cài yòu hào chī yòu piányí. — This dish is both delicious and cheap.

**5. 虽然…但是… (suīrán…dànshì…) — although…but…**
- 虽然我很累，但是我很开心。Suīrán wǒ hěn lèi, dànshì wǒ hěn kāixīn. — Although I'm tired, I'm very happy.

**6. 因为…所以… (yīnwèi…suǒyǐ…) — because…therefore…**
- 因为下雨，所以我没出去。Yīnwèi xià yǔ, suǒyǐ wǒ méi chūqù. — Because it rained, I didn't go out.

**7. 如果…就… (rúguǒ…jiù…) — if…then…**
- 如果你来，我就很高兴。Rúguǒ nǐ lái, wǒ jiù hěn gāoxìng. — If you come, I'll be very happy.

```
1=一 2=二 3=三 4=四 5=五 6=六 7=七 8=八 9=九 10=十
11=十一  20=二十  21=二十一  100=一百  1000=一千  10000=一万
```

## Tone Summary
```
1st tone (ā): high flat     →  妈 mā (mom)
2nd tone (á): rising        →  麻 má (numb)
3rd tone (ǎ): dip then rise →  马 mǎ (horse)
4th tone (à): falling       →  骂 mà (scold)
Neutral (a):  short, light  →  吗 ma (?)
```
